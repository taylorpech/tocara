1. Section 8 (Housing Choice Voucher Program)

  • Purpose: Rental assistance for low-income families.
  • Status Check/Application (Milwaukee):
    • Administered by the Housing Authority of the City of Milwaukee (HACM).
    • HACM General Phone: (414) 286-5650.
    • HACM Rent Assistance Program (Section 8) Office: 5011 W. Lisbon Ave., Milwaukee, WI 53210.
    • Current Status:
      • The Housing Choice Voucher (Section 8) waiting list remains CLOSED.
      • The Public Housing waiting list opened March 5, 2025.
      • For Project-Based Rent Assistance (for specific buildings), check www.hacmpbreg.com or call (414) 286-5500 for registration days.
    • Existing application status: waitlistcheck.com (may require specific login details from HACM).
  • Eligibility: Income limits, family definition, SSN/eligible immigrant status, criminal background checks (may vary).

2. SSI (Supplemental Security Income) Based on Disability

  • Purpose: Financial assistance for disabled individuals with limited income/resources.
  • Application Methods (Federal Program):
    • Online: ssa.gov/applyfordisability/index.html.
    • Phone: Social Security Administration at 1-800-772-1213 (TTY 1-800-325-0778).
    • In-person (Milwaukee SSA Offices):
      • 6300 W Fond Du Lac Ave, Milwaukee, WI
      • 8455 W Layton Ave, Greenfield, WI
      • 310 W Wisconsin Ave #260, Milwaukee, WI
      • 5020 W North Ave, Milwaukee, WI
  • Tip: Call date may be used as application filing date if appointment is kept. SSA assists with forms.

4. Cash App vs. Bank Account for Direct Deposit

Recommendation: A traditional bank account or credit union account is generally safer for receiving government benefits due to FDIC insurance and consumer protections.

Cash App Pros:

  • Faster access to funds (up to 2 days early).
  • No monthly fees.
  • Easy mobile app use.

Cash App Cons:

  • Funds are NOT directly FDIC insured (though partner banks are).
  • Less robust customer service.
  • Payment limits.
  • No interest earned.
  • Potential for disrupted access.

Traditional Bank Account Pros:

  • FDIC insured (up to $250,000) for security.
  • Robust customer service.
  • Multiple access points (ATMs, branches, online).
  • Access to other financial services.

Traditional Bank Account Cons:

  • May have some fees (free options often available with direct deposit).
  • Slightly slower access to funds.

5. Taxes (2022, 2023, 2024)

  • Action: Gather all income statements (W-2s, 1099s, etc.) for each year. SSI is generally not taxable.
  • Filing Options:
    • IRS Free File: If income qualifies.
    • Tax Software: Purchase software for specific past years.
    • Paper Filing: Download forms from IRS website and mail.
  • Assistance (Wisconsin): Consider VITA (Volunteer Income Tax Assistance) or TCE (Tax Counseling for the Elderly) programs for free help.
    • Find VITA sites: Call (800) 906-9887 or visit irs.gov (search for VITA).
    • Find TCE sites: Call (888) 227-7669 or visit aarp.org/money/taxes/aarp_taxaide/.
    • You can also call "211" for a site near you in Wisconsin.
